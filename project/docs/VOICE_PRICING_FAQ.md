# Voice Pricing FAQ (Customer Objection Handling)

## 1) Why is pricing not a simple per-minute multiplication?
Linear minute pricing creates unfair jumps and often breaks package logic. We use range-based packages so pricing remains predictable while preserving quality, revision scope, and delivery reliability.

## 2) Why would I choose Layer B over pure AI tools?
Layer B is designed for continuity: traceable voice workflow, patch compatibility, and governance clarity. Pure AI output may be fast, but continuity across later updates can become costly and inconsistent.

## 3) If Layer B can get expensive, why not always choose Layer C?
Layer C is built for premium performance control and higher-stakes execution. Layer B is optimized for continuity and scalable brand operations. They solve different risk profiles, not the same need at different quality levels.

## 4) Are you charging more just because a real person is involved?
No. Pricing reflects delivery accountability and operational risk management: continuity, revision control, licensing clarity, and production reliability. Human involvement is one component, not the only component.

## 5) What makes your service higher value than "get one clip and leave" platforms?
We support long-term voice lifecycle management. The same voice can be extended through controlled updates, with clearer rights and lower re-casting friction for ongoing campaigns.

## 6) How do I know which layer fits my project?
Use decision questions:
- Is speed the top priority? Start with Layer A.
- Is brand voice continuity required across future updates? Choose Layer B.
- Is this a high-visibility release with low risk tolerance? Choose Layer C.

## 7) How do you prevent pricing from feeling inconsistent?
We validate ladder integrity and boundary smoothness before launch:
- middle-tier common cases must not collide with premium entry,
- range transitions should avoid abrupt pricing cliffs.

## 8) Can I start at a lower layer and upgrade later?
Yes. Package design includes explicit escalation paths so projects can move upward when requirements change.
